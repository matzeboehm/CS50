The first law of thermodynamics is an especially relevant physical law for my day to day life. Derived from mechanics it describes that different kinds of energies can be converted into other kinds of energies. It also states that energy can not be generated or lost, it just exists in different forms.

The simplest form is described as seen in equation 1. The change of internal energy \(\Delta U\) is the sum of the change of heat \(\Delta Q\) and the work performed by the system \(\Delta W\).

\[\Delta U = {\Delta Q + \Delta W}   (1) \]

The second law of thermodynamics describes that processes can only be performed in one flowing direction. For example, electricity turns to heat on a stove and the water in the pot starts to boil. However, if turn the stove of, the boiling water will not cause a flow of current in the stove.
